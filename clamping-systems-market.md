<p><p>Clamping systems are classified into several types based on their operation. Manual clamping involves using hand tools for securing workpieces, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness. Pneumatic clamping utilizes compressed air for quick and efficient clamping, ideal for high-speed applications. Hydraulic clamping employs fluid pressure to provide strong holding force, making it suitable for heavy-duty tasks. Other types may include magnetic or vacuum clamping systems, each serving specific needs in various industrial contexts, enhancing efficiency and precision in manufacturing processes.</p></p>
