<p><p>The hazardous location thermostat market is divided into two main types: line-voltage and low-voltage thermostats. Line-voltage thermostats operate directly on higher voltages, typically used for controlling electric heating systems in hazardous environments, ensuring reliable temperature regulation. Low-voltage thermostats, on the other hand, work with lower voltage systems, often used in conjunction with transformers or control circuits, tailored for specific applications in potentially explosive atmospheres. Both types prioritize safety and compliance with industry standards.</p></p>
